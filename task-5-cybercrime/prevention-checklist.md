# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: The Student Checklist

A guide for college students in India to navigate the digital world safely. Use this checklist to audit your digital hygiene and financial security.

---

## 💻 General Digital Safety
* **[ ] Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Set up MFA (or 2FA) on your university portals, Gmail, and social media. Even if someone phishes your password, they cannot log in without the one-time code on your phone.
* **[ ] Avoid Sensitive Tasks on Public Wi-Fi:** Never log into your bank or student portal using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a **VPN** to prevent "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks where hackers intercept your data.
* **[ ] Patch Your Software:** Don't ignore OS or app update notifications. These often contain critical "security patches" that fix vulnerabilities currently being exploited by hackers.
* **[ ] Spot the "Registration Fee" Scam:** Be skeptical of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering high-paying part-time jobs or scholarships that require a "processing fee." **Legitimate companies never ask for money to hire you.**
* **[ ] Private Profiles Only:** Keep your Instagram and Facebook profiles private. Sharing your location or "Check-ins" in real-time informs strangers of your physical movements and when your hostel room might be empty.
* **[ ] Secure Your IDs:** Do not store photos of your Aadhaar, PAN card, or marksheets in your phone's unencrypted gallery. Use **DigiLocker** or a password-protected vault instead.

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
* **[ ] The "Receive" Rule:** You **never** need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If a buyer on an online marketplace (like OLX) asks you to scan a code to "get paid," it is a scam.
* **[ ] Verify the Legal Name:** Before hitting 'Pay' on GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, look at the legal name associated with the UPI ID. Fraudsters often use deceptive display names (e.g., "Official_College_Account") to hide their personal bank details.

---

## 🚨 How to Report a Cyber Crime

If you have been targeted by a scam or a cyber-offense, speed is your best defense.

* **📞 Immediate Helpline:** Call **1930** immediately. For financial fraud, reporting within the "Golden Hour" (the first 2 hours) significantly increases the chance of freezing the money before the scammer withdraws it.
* **💻 Official Portal:** File a formal complaint and upload evidence (screenshots/URLs) at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)**.

> **Pro Tip:** Always keep a record of transaction IDs, chat logs, and the mobile numbers used by the fraudsters to assist the investigation.
